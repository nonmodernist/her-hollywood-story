Pattern Analysis

Data-driven explorations of Hollywood's adaptation ecosystem

With over 1,125 films adapted from more than 490 American women writers, our dataset reveals patterns sometimes overlooked by traditional film history. As a complement to the browsable database, our "pattern" pages combine quantitative analysis with archival research to illuminate how women's literature shaped Hollywood's business practices, genre conventions, and star system.

Each pattern page uses custom data visualizations, statistical analysis, and targeted case studies to transform raw data into scholarly insights.

Published Analyses

The Twenty-Timers Club

5 authors • 136 films • 47 years

Five women whose works were adapted 20+ times each, accounting for 12% of all Hollywood adaptations from American women writers. Interactive network analysis reveals how these authors dominated specific genres and studio relationships across five decades.

Features: Timeline analysis, studio distribution charts, network visualization, decade-by-decade breakdown

America's Sweetheart Chooses Her Stories

17 films • 13 women authors • A singular legacy

How Mary Pickford strategically chose women's stories to build her empire, from Victorian melodramas to photoplay editions to the Shirley Temple licensing deals. Traces the actress-producer's transformation of literary properties into multimedia assets.

Features: Publication timeline analysis, comparative remakes study, photoplay edition documentation

What These Patterns Reveal

Concentration of Power

Just 1% of adapted writers generated 12% of all films. This extreme concentration reveals how Hollywood's "meritocracy" actually functioned through established relationships and proven formulas.

Genre Evolution

Women's literature didn't just provide content—it established entire genres. From the "women's picture" melodrama to mystery conventions, these patterns trace genre genealogies back to their literary sources.

Media Convergence

Magazine serialization, book publication, film adaptation, photoplay editions—our data captures the full lifecycle of transmedia storytelling decades before the term existed.

Star System Mechanics

By tracking which stories launched careers and which sustained them, we see how literary material functioned as star-making machinery, particularly for actresses transitioning to production.

Business Innovation

From author-owned studios to remake rights management, these patterns document early experiments in intellectual property control that presaged modern entertainment conglomerates.

Cultural Memory

Which stories survived generational transitions? Which vanished? The patterns reveal how Hollywood's adaptation choices shaped American cultural memory of women's literature.

Our Analytical Approach

Each pattern analysis combines multiple methodological approaches to transform database records into scholarly arguments:

Quantitative Analysis

We use statistical methods to identify significant patterns, from simple frequency distributions to network analysis. Visualizations are created using Chart.js and Cytoscape.js, allowing interactive exploration of complex relationships.

Archival Research

Database patterns point us toward specific archival questions. We consult trade publications, studio records, author papers, and contemporary sources to contextualize our quantitative findings.

Case Study Selection

Within each pattern, we identify exemplary cases that illuminate broader trends. These micro-histories ground our macro-analysis in specific production contexts and individual decisions.

Cross-Pattern Connections

Patterns aren't isolated—they intersect and inform each other. The Twenty-Timers dominated rapid adaptations; Mary Pickford's choices influenced remake cycles. These connections reveal the systemic nature of Hollywood's adaptation practices.

For Researchers

Citation-Ready: All pattern pages include complete methodological notes, data sources, and ready-to-copy citations. Visualizations include figure numbers and explanatory captions suitable for academic use.

Database Integration: Pattern pages link directly to relevant database searches, allowing you to explore the underlying data. Every claim is traceable back to specific films, authors, and works.

Transparent Limitations: We document what we don't know as carefully as what we do. Each analysis acknowledges data gaps, preservation issues, and methodological constraints.

Reproducible Findings: Our open database and documented methods mean other scholars can verify our patterns and build upon our analysis. We welcome challenges, corrections, and extensions of our work.
